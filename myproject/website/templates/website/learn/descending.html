Descending Graph

The descending triangle is a bearish continuation pattern that forms on a price chart, characterized by a horizontal support line and a downward-sloping trendline for resistance. The horizontal support line is formed by connecting multiple price lows, while the downward-sloping trendline is formed by connecting lower highs. The pattern indicates that sellers are gradually gaining strength as price consolidates near the support level, and a breakout below the support line is anticipated. Confirmation of the pattern is sought through a breakout below the support line, accompanied by an increase in trading volume, indicating potential increased selling pressure. Price targets can be estimated by measuring the height of the triangle pattern and subtracting it from the breakout point, while implementing proper risk management techniques and considering other technical indicators or fundamental analysis for confirmation.
