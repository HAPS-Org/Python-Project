Head n Shoulders Graph

The head and shoulders pattern is a bearish reversal pattern that is often seen in stock charts. The pattern is formed when the stock price rises to a peak (the left shoulder), then drops and rises again to a higher peak (the head), and then drops again to a level similar to the left shoulder (the right shoulder). The neckline is a horizontal line that connects the lows of the left and right shoulders. Traders often look for confirmation of the pattern by waiting for a breakout below the neckline, which signals a potential continuation of the stock's downward trend. Proper risk management, including setting stop-loss orders and using position sizing, is essential when trading head and shoulders patterns, as there is always a risk of a false breakout or reversal. It is also important to note that the pattern is not foolproof and may not work in all market conditions.
