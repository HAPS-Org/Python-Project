Gaps Graph

A gap is a discontinuity in a stock's price chart that occurs when the current day's opening price is higher or lower than the previous day's closing price. There are three types of gaps: common gaps, breakaway gaps, and runaway gaps. Common gaps occur within the trading range and are typically caused by normal market fluctuations. Breakaway gaps occur when the stock price breaks out of a trading range or a chart pattern, such as a trendline or a support/resistance level. Runaway gaps occur in the middle of a trend and are often caused by a significant event, such as an earnings announcement or news release, that causes a sudden shift in market sentiment. Proper risk management, including setting stop-loss orders and using position sizing, is essential when trading gaps, as there is always a risk of price volatility and sudden price movements. It is also important to note that gaps may not always be filled, and they may indicate a change in market sentiment or direction.
