Wedge Graph

The wedge pattern is a common chart pattern that can be bullish or bearish, characterized by converging trendlines forming a wedge shape. Wedges can be ascending or descending, forming during a period of consolidation and indecision in the market. Wedges are continuation patterns, signaling a temporary pause before the price trend resumes in the direction of the preceding trend. Confirmation of the pattern is sought through a breakout above or below the converging trendlines, accompanied by sustained price movement and volume analysis. Price targets can be estimated using the height of the wedge pattern as a measure, from the widest part of the wedge to the breakout point, while using proper risk management techniques and considering other technical indicators or fundamental analysis for confirmation.
