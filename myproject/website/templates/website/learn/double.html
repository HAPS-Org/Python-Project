Double Graph

The double top pattern is a bearish reversal pattern that is formed when the stock price reaches a high, pulls back, rises again to a similar high, and then drops below the previous low. The double bottom pattern is a bullish reversal pattern that is formed when the stock price reaches a low, bounces back, drops again to a similar low, and then rises above the previous high. In both patterns, the two highs or lows form a resistance or support level, respectively. Traders often look for confirmation of the pattern by waiting for a breakout below the support level in the double top pattern or above the resistance level in the double bottom pattern. Proper risk management, including setting stop-loss orders and using position sizing, is essential when trading double top and double bottom patterns, as there is always a risk of a false breakout or reversal. It is also important to note that the pattern is not foolproof and may not work in all market conditions.
